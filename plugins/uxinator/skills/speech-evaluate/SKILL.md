# Speech Evaluate

Post-delivery retrospective for keynotes and conference talks. Assesses whether the provocation landed, the emotional arc worked, and the audience shifted. Feeds directly into speech-iterate for versioning the talk.

## Input Types

- **Post-talk notes or transcript** (primary)
- **Audience feedback** (surveys, comments, social media, hallway conversations)
- **Tim's self-assessment** (what he felt during delivery)
- **Recording** (if available)
- **Talk structure from speech-design** (to compare planned vs. actual)

## Evaluation Framework

### 1. The Provocation Test

```
DID THE AUDIENCE SHIFT?
───────────────────────
BEFORE STATE: [What the audience believed/felt before the talk]
INTENDED AFTER STATE: [What they should believe/feel after]
ACTUAL AFTER STATE: [Evidence of what actually happened]

SHIFT ACHIEVED: [YES | PARTIALLY | NO]

EVIDENCE:
- [Quote, behavior, feedback that shows shift]
- [Quote, behavior, feedback that shows shift]
- [Or: silence / polite applause / generic "that was great" = NO shift evidence]
```

### 2. The Repeatable Line Test

```
THE ONE LINE
────────────
DESIGNED LINE:    [What was supposed to be repeated]
DID ANYONE REPEAT IT? [YES — where? | NO]
WHAT THEY ACTUALLY REPEATED: [Sometimes a different line lands harder]

IF DIFFERENT: Consider making THAT line the designed one in v2.
```

### 3. Peak-End Assessment

```
PEAK-END EVALUATION
───────────────────
DESIGNED PEAK:    [What was planned as the peak moment]
ACTUAL PEAK:      [What the audience responded to most intensely]
MATCH:            [SAME | DIFFERENT — note what surprised]

DESIGNED CLOSE:   [What was planned]
ACTUAL CLOSE:     [What Tim actually said/did]
CLOSE QUALITY:    [STRONG | ADEQUATE | TRAILED OFF | DIFFERENT FROM PLAN]

PEAK-END PREDICTION: [Based on actual peak + actual end, what will
                       the audience REMEMBER about this talk?]
```

### 4. Mode Drift Review

```
MODE DISCIPLINE
───────────────
PLANNED MODE:      KEYNOTE (provocation)
ACTUAL MODE:       [What Tim actually did — may vary by section]

DRIFT MOMENTS:
- [Timestamp/section]: [What happened — e.g., "Stopped to explain the
  UX² framework for 4 minutes. Audience energy dropped visibly."]

EXPLAIN CONTAMINATION: [X]% of delivery was education, not provocation
```

### 5. Audience Signals

```
AUDIENCE RESPONSE DATA
──────────────────────
DURING TALK:
- Energy shifts:     [When did they lean in? When did they check phones?]
- Laughter:          [Where? Was it earned or polite?]
- Note-taking:       [Where? This indicates "useful" not "moved"]
- Visible reactions: [Nods, head shakes, whispers to neighbor]

AFTER TALK:
- Approach rate:     [How many people came up to Tim after?]
- Questions asked:   [What did they ask? "How do I..." = training response.
                      "I never thought of it that way" = provocation response.]
- Social mentions:   [Any posts, tags, quotes shared?]
- Feedback forms:    [Scores + verbatim comments]
- Invitations:       [Did anyone invite Tim to speak again? Where?]
- Connections:       [LinkedIn requests, card exchanges, DMs]

SIGNAL INTERPRETATION:
[What do these signals tell us about whether the talk provoked or taught?]
```

### 6. Tim's Self-Assessment

```
TIM'S FELT EXPERIENCE
─────────────────────
Energy level:        [How did Tim feel during delivery? Alive? Flat? Rushed?]
Story delivery:      [Did the anchor story land the way he wanted?]
Close execution:     [Did Tim deliver the close as designed or improvise?]
Drift awareness:     [Did Tim notice himself drifting? Where?]
Audience connection: [Did Tim feel connected to the room or performing at it?]
Surprise moments:    [Anything unexpected that worked? That didn't?]
```

### 7. Verdict

```
KEYNOTE EVALUATION
══════════════════
PROVOCATION:     [LANDED | PARTIALLY | MISSED]
EMOTIONAL ARC:   [COMPELLING | ADEQUATE | FLAT]
CLOSE:           [STRONG | ADEQUATE | WEAK]
MODE DISCIPLINE: [CLEAN | MINOR DRIFT | MAJOR DRIFT]
AUDIENCE SHIFT:  [EVIDENT | UNCLEAR | NO EVIDENCE]

OVERALL: [X/50 across 5 dimensions, 10 each]

WHAT WORKED (keep for v2):
1. [Specific]
2. [Specific]

WHAT DIDN'T (fix for v2):
1. [Specific]
2. [Specific]

VERSION NOTE: [What should change in the next delivery of this talk?
               Feed this into speech-iterate.]
```

## Cross-Skill Integration

- **From speech-design**: Compare designed structure against actual delivery
- **From speech-rehearse**: Compare rehearsal feedback against actual performance
- **Into speech-iterate**: Evaluation findings drive versioning decisions
- **Into thought-leadership-librarian**: Strong audience responses indicate concept resonance

## What This Skill Does NOT Do

- Evaluate training sessions (use instructional-designer skills)
- Evaluate strategy sessions (use strategy-evaluate)
- Redesign the talk (use speech-iterate → speech-design)
- Coach delivery (use speech-rehearse)
