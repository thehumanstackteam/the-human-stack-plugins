# Engagement Toolkit

A library of 10 engagement moves, each with exact scripts Tim can use. All designed for ease of execution and riff-ability.

---

## 1. Pair-Share

**When:** After introducing a framework, concept, or challenge. Best for processing.
**Setup time:** 30 seconds
**Say This:**
> "Turn to the person next to you. What's one [concept/problem/example] from what we just talked about that shows up in your org? 90 seconds. Go."

**Timer:** 90 sec - 2 min
**Tim note:** He'll want to extend this. Set a clear timer on your phone. When it goes off, have one pair report back (30 sec harvest).

---

## 2. Raise-Hand Poll

**When:** Quick pulse check or to validate a point. Wakes up a passive room.
**Setup time:** 15 seconds
**Say This:**
> "Raise your hand if you've ever [experience]. Keep your hand up. Yeah. [Pause.] That's the problem we're about to solve."

**Timer:** 30 sec
**Tim note:** Use the hands to transition into your next point, not as a quiz. The gesture is the point, not the count.

---

## 3. Cascading Poll

**When:** Mid-session energy dip. Creates a visual and usually gets a laugh.
**Setup time:** 20 seconds
**Say This:**
> "Raise your hand if you've tried to [common approach]. Keep it up if it actually [result]. Keep it up if it only worked because [harder condition]."

**Timer:** 45 sec
**Tim note:** The hands go down at each step, creating a visual funnel. It's funny and makes a point in one motion.

---

## 4. One-Word Harvest

**When:** Transitions. Bridges between segments. Collects audience material Tim can build from.
**Setup time:** 15 seconds
**Say This:**
> "Give me one word for what you just [experienced/noticed/felt] in that exercise. Just shout it out. One word."

**Timer:** 1-2 min
**Tim note:** Collect 4-5 words max. Write them down if you can. Then: "Those words are exactly why we're moving to [next topic]." You can riff off those words.

---

## 5. Think-Write-Share

**When:** Deeper processing. After a story, difficult concept, or moment that needs reflection.
**Setup time:** 1 minute
**Say This:**
> "Take a minute. Silently think about [specific prompt]. Then jot down one sentence or one phrase. When I count to three, turn to someone near you and share what you wrote. Ready? One, two, three."

**Timer:** 3-4 min total (1 min think, 1 min write, 2 min pair-share)
**Tim note:** The writing step is crucial — it forces an answer Tim didn't dictate. You get more real material to riff.

---

## 6. Stand If

**When:** Physical reset mid-session. Best after 15-20 min of sitting.
**Setup time:** 10 seconds
**Say This:**
> "Everybody stand up if you've ever [experience]. Stay standing if you've [condition]. Okay, sit back down."

**Timer:** 30 sec
**Tim note:** Don't make it a test. Make it relatable and a little funny. The standing breaks lethargy.

---

## 7. Chat Waterfall (Virtual Sessions)

**When:** Virtual rooms, mid-session. Creates energy through simultaneous response.
**Setup time:** 20 seconds
**Say This:**
> "Type your answer in the chat right now, but don't hit send. I'll give you a countdown. On three, everyone sends at once. One, two, three — send!"

**Timer:** 1 min
**Tim note:** The waterfall of responses in the chat at once creates momentum. You can scan and pick out themes to riff off.

---

## 8. Spectrum Line

**When:** Nuanced topics. "Where are you on this spectrum?" Works in-person or virtual.
**Setup time:** 20 seconds
**Say This:**
> "On a scale of 1 to 10, where are you with [topic]? Hold up your fingers. Don't worry about what anyone else shows. Just your honest number. Look around. See the range we have in here?"

**Timer:** 30 sec
**Tim note:** The visual of different fingers creates immediate conversation. You can ask a few people at different numbers why they're there.

---

## 9. Story Prompt

**When:** Personal connection. After a model or framework. Best for deep content.
**Setup time:** 30 seconds
**Say This:**
> "Think of a time when [specific situation happened to you]. You've got 90 seconds. Tell the person next to you. What happened? What did you do? Go."

**Timer:** 2 min
**Tim note:** Personal stories stick. And hearing peer stories (not from you) creates buy-in. Let it run; people will want to share.

---

## 10. Prediction

**When:** Curiosity hook. Before revealing a finding, next step, or answer.
**Setup time:** 15 seconds
**Say This:**
> "Before I show you what happened next, what do you think happens? What's your prediction? Talk to someone near you for 30 seconds."

**Timer:** 30 sec
**Tim note:** Prediction creates cognitive engagement. People are primed to learn the "real" answer. You can call out a prediction and say "exactly" or "interesting — here's what actually occurred."

---

## Mix & Match Notes

- In a 60-minute session, use 3-4 different moves. Repeat one type once only if the session is 90+ min.
- Alternate between individual reflection (think-write), pair work (pair-share, story), and full-room polls (raise hand, stand if).
- Harvest moments (one-word, prediction reveal) should happen right after interaction — don't let silence sit.
- Physical engagement (stand if, spectrum line) should come in the second half of a session, not the first 10 min.
